Analyze the audio spectrogram for specific patterns:

Identify if the high-frequency region (4-6kHz) has a weak, narrow-band pattern of approximately 2kHz width.
Observe if weak horizontal patterns exist in the mid to high-frequency regions (5-7kHz), with a reduction in intensity over 1-2kHz.
Look for irregular horizontal patterns, especially if intensity decreases at higher frequencies.
Check for regular patterns across time and frequency:

Verify if regular vertical patterns are present across all bands for 1-10 seconds.
Confirm the presence of strong energy in low-frequency bands.
Detect consistent patterns in the low-frequency spectrum.
Evaluate short-term changes in frequency intensity:

Determine if the mid-frequency intensity increases slightly over a 0.5-second period.
Look for irregular, moderate-intensity patterns in the mid to high-frequency range over 0.5 seconds.
Examine vertical intensity patterns:

Identify strong vertical patterns in the low to mid-frequency range.
Check for multiple irregular vertical patterns of varying intensities across all frequency bands.
Observe for spike-like patterns:

Detect weak or irregular spike patterns extending from low to high frequencies.
Confirm if spike patterns in higher frequencies gradually diminish in intensity.
Assess overall energy distribution:

Analyze if the intensity increases uniformly from 0kHz to 8kHz.
Confirm if energy distribution is consistently stronger in higher voltage (40V) conditions compared to lower voltage (34V).
